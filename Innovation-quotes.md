If I were picking the **10 best quotes** from David’s highlights on innovation, experimenting, and trial-and-error, I’d bias toward the ones that are both **memorable** and **useful as operating principles**.

## Top 10

1. **“Innovation requires builders not bean-counters.”**  
From [Against the Odds](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1558/?highlight=45464)  
Why it stands out: brutally concise. It captures the idea that innovation is made by people who build, test, and ship—not by people optimizing spreadsheets from a distance.

2. **“Failure and invention are inseparable twins.”**  
From [Working Backwards](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1485/?highlight=51333)  
This is probably the clearest statement in David’s highlights that experimentation is not optional if you want invention. If you remove failure, you usually remove novelty too.

3. **“In a company that was born to innovate, the risk is in not innovating… The real risk is to think it is safe to play it safe.”**  
From [Jony Ive](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1487/?highlight=51429)  
A great reminder that in dynamic industries, caution can be the most dangerous strategy.

4. **“By always asking why we are doing this or that, we can find new paths… The desire to experiment must continually lead us forward.”**  
From [Leading by Design](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1509/?highlight=48477)  
This is one of the best process-oriented quotes in David’s highlights: progress starts with questioning defaults.

5. **“Experts tend to be confident that they have all the answers and, because of this trait, they can kill new ideas.”**  
From [Invention](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1451/?highlight=49667)  
One of the deepest recurring themes in David’s highlights is that orthodoxy often suffocates invention. New ideas usually look wrong before they look inevitable.

6. **“Learning by doing. Learning by trial and error. Learning by failing. These are all effective forms of education.”**  
From [Invention](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1451/?highlight=49684)  
This is less flashy, but maybe more important than the others. It turns experimentation into a philosophy of learning, not just product development.

7. **“The general’s view of the world as one of constant competition, led to his belief that innovation, continuous innovation, never relaxing, was the only way to stay ahead of the competition.”**  
From [Creative Capital](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1452/?highlight=49761)  
A strong quote because it frames innovation not as an event but as a permanent posture.

8. **“The whole point of moving things forward is that you run into problems, failures, things that don’t work. You need to back up and try again.”**  
From [Invent and Wander](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1575/?highlight=54697)  
This is a very Bezos-like idea: progress is not the absence of setbacks; progress is your ability to iterate through them.

9. **“An essential aspect of creativity is not being afraid to fail.”**  
From [A Triumph of Genius: Edwin Land, Polaroid, and the Kodak Patent War](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1603/?highlight=56234)  
This is one of the strongest quotes in David’s highlights because it connects creativity directly to emotional tolerance for repeated failure.

10. **“The world isn’t waiting for more of the same.”**  
From [The Creative Act](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/3307/?highlight=90473)  
Short, sharp, and true. Innovation starts with refusing to merely repeat.

## My 3 favorites

If you want the **most powerful three**, I’d pick:

- **“Innovation requires builders not bean-counters.”** from [Against the Odds](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1558/?highlight=45464)
- **“Failure and invention are inseparable twins.”** from [Working Backwards](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1485/?highlight=51333)
- **“The real risk is to think it is safe to play it safe.”** from [Jony Ive](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1487/?highlight=51429)

Those three together almost form a complete theory:
- innovation is done by builders,
- builders must experiment,
- experimentation implies failure,
- therefore avoiding failure is often avoiding innovation.

## Best quote if you want something more poetic

If you want something a little less managerial and more inspiring, this is one of the best from David’s highlights:

- **“The most radical and transformative of inventions are often those that empower others to unleash their creativity—to pursue their dreams.”** from [Invent and Wander](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1575/?highlight=54709)

That one is great because it defines innovation not just as novelty, but as creating tools that multiply other people’s abilities.

## Best quote if you want a rule for action

For practical use, I’d probably choose:

- **“To invent you have to experiment, and if you know in advance that it’s going to work, it’s not an experiment.”**  
Referenced in David’s transcript on [Jeff Bezos' Shareholder Letters. All of them.](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2016/?location=01:24:14)

That’s one of the cleanest decision rules you can use in work and life.
