Executive verdict · Composite 66/100

Eight distinct properties. One Instagram-famous hotel. Seven invisible operators.

Paradiso is genuinely AI-cited for Art Deco Ibiza. Cubanito owns the Cuban-themed niche so completely that it is essentially uncontested. The other six properties are each pulling a unique cultural thesis (tropical Art Deco, rock, Route 66, fashion designers, cinema, Italian Dolce Vita) but landing as invisible to the engines that increasingly choose where guests stay. The parent surface, theconcepthotels.com, is a portal to eight stories yet tells none of them at group level. The men who designed all of this, Diego Calvo and Tallyn Planells, do not appear on their own website. Fix that, fix four-way brand-name fragmentation, fix the social link parity, and the group converts a 66 composite into the 80+ that the design quality already justifies.

W1 · Brand Architecture

Mongibello is treated as a sister brand on the parent site, with its own logo, its own domain (mongibelloibiza.com) and a Preferred Hotels & Resorts affiliation. The other seven sit beneath The Concept Hotels umbrella with shared template treatment. As of April 2026, Mongibello was promoted from 4-star to 5-star, formally separating it from the rest. The diagram below reflects the architecture as designed.

Architecture finding · design intent vs digital reality

The founders intentionally separated Mongibello as a sister brand. The nav respects that. The logo respects that. The domain split respects that. But the parent site footer treats all eight identically, social handles are linked inconsistently and there is no architecture page explaining why Mongibello carries its own star rating. A sceptical reader of theconcepthotels.com cannot tell that the architecture is deliberate. It looks accidental. Add a one-paragraph architecture explainer to the parent footer and the design intent reads.

W4 · Group Google Business Profile audit

Six structural defects on the group GBP

  1. Miscategorised as “Corporate office.” Should be Hotel chain or Hospitality. As a corporate office the listing is locked out of the local hotel pack for every Ibiza search. Single highest-leverage fix on this page.
  2. 45 reviews total across an 8-property group. Industry health norm is at least ten reviews per property. The group is sitting at roughly six. Each property GBP is likely undernourished too.
  3. “Reviews aren’t verified” flag visible on the knowledge panel. Visible signal of low engagement; suppresses click-through.
  4. Spanish-only description. The English-speaking guest, the actual ideal customer profile (ICP) for design-led party Ibiza, sees nothing on the highest-trust surface in search.
  5. No Q&A populated. Empty Q&A invites third parties to answer. Two of the existing visible questions are answered by random users.
  6. Photo set thin for an 8-property group. Exterior present, interior thin, property-level coverage absent.

Total fix effort across all six items is half a day for someone with edit access to the GBP. The category fix alone (one click) re-enables local pack eligibility for the parent group across every Ibiza accommodation query.

W5 · Brand-name consistency audit

Three trading-name variants, before the legal entity even enters

Crawling the parent site, the corporate site and the social bios surfaces three variants of the trading name in regular use. AI engines treat each as a candidate canonical entity. They cannot pick one, so the citation density splits across all three.

  • “The Concept Hotels” in the H1, the URL slug, the social handle of one IG account and the Mongibello footer reference to concept_hotel_group.
  • “Concept Hotel Group” on concepthotelgroup.es, the Spanish corporate domain, and the Google Knowledge Panel.
  • “Concept Hotels Group” appears inconsistently on subpages within theconcepthotels.com itself.

Pick one. Make every public surface match. The simplest decision is to lead with Concept Hotel Group (matches GBP and the legal-entity short form) and treat The Concept Hotels as the consumer trading brand for the 7-property collection sitting beneath it. Mongibello stays as a named sister brand.
