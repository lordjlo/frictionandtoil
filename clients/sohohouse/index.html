Primary Finding

Brand Giant, SEO Afterthought

Soho House dominates through PR and word-of-mouth while organic search is ceded entirely to editorial roundups — the brand appears in those lists as a subject, not as the ranking site. With a 112,000-person waitlist, search may feel irrelevant, but category queries for "private members club" [city] are captured by competitors who will eventually convert applicants who can't get in. Schema is absent, meta descriptions are missing on key pages, and AI assistant queries on ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are likely answered by editorial sources rather than sohohouse.com itself. Key Findings by Dimension

Search · 50

No Schema, No Meta Descriptions, No Category Rankings

Key pages — About and Membership — returned no meta descriptions. No schema markup detected across the homepage or inner pages. The site does not rank page 1 for "private members club London" — editorial sites dominate those results.

Design · 90

Luxury UX That Sets the Category Standard

Aspirational above-fold value proposition, clear primary CTAs, minimalist luxury visual hierarchy, and consistent brand application across pages. The H1 prioritises brand poetry over keyword targeting — a deliberate choice with an SEO trade-off.

Brand · 85

Strong Identity, Blurring at the Edges

Founder (Nick Jones) and CEO (Andrew Carnie) are named. Scale is quantified — 48 Houses, 19 countries, 8,000+ artworks, 112k waitlist (as of early 2025). "Club for creatives" differentiates but remains broad; the site never sharpens to name which creative industries it serves first.

Compete · 50

Invisible in Category Search, Pricing Hidden

No pricing visible on homepage or membership page — "Calculate pricing" gates the figure. Category keyword searches are dominated by Annabel's, The Arts Club, and editorial roundups. No evidence of competitor bidding defence on brand terms.

Growth · 80

Strong Funnel, Friction at the Pricing Gate

Newsletter capture, multiple CTAs, and a clear application path are all in place. The waitlist model (112,000+ applicants as of early 2025) validates demand. The missing piece: a transparency lever for non-applicants — no indication of what membership costs until users self-select into the funnel.
