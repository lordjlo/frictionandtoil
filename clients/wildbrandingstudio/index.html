What Each Dimension Covers
Plain English explanations — no jargon

Search — Can people find you on Google and in AI answers?

This measures how visible your website is when someone searches for brand designers, wellness branding, or photography studios. It covers the technical setup of your site, the quality of your page content, how trusted your site is, and — critically for 2026 — whether AI engines like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity can read and cite you when buyers ask them for recommendations.

Think of it as: if someone searched "brand designer for wellness practitioners UK" or asked ChatGPT the same question right now, would they find you? A low score means probably not.

Design — Does your site look the part?

This assesses whether your website's visual presentation matches the quality of work you produce. It covers layout and structure, visual identity, colour choices, typography, and motion. For a brand identity studio, the bar is highest here — your own site is your portfolio.

Think of it as: if a buyer compared your site against the brand identity work in your portfolio, would the site reinforce the same quality — or let it down?

Brand — Are you saying the right things, consistently?

This looks at whether your brand communicates clearly and consistently across every page. It covers the tone and quality of your writing, how visible you are across digital channels, how well you're positioned, and whether the experience feels joined-up across services, portfolio, and blog.

Think of it as: does your website make it immediately obvious what makes Wild Branding Studio different — and does it say that confidently every time?

Compete — How do you stand against your competitors?

This maps your position against direct competitors (Monroe Creative, Be More You Branding, Designbury, Neon Rocks). It looks at what they do well, where they're vulnerable, what gap you could own, and whether you're differentiated enough for a buyer to choose you over them.

Think of it as: if a buyer looked at your site and a competitor's side by side, what would make them choose you — and is that obvious?

Growth — Is your site set up to win new clients?

This evaluates whether your site has the infrastructure to attract visitors and convert them into discovery calls and bookings. It covers your ideal customer profile, your traffic strategy, your conversion copy, and the metrics that tell you what's working.

Think of it as: beyond word-of-mouth from existing clients, is your website actively generating new enquiries? A low score means it probably isn't — and the report shows you exactly why.

How long will these improvements take to show results? ▼

How accurate are these scores? ▼
Each score is calculated against industry benchmarks — W3C technical standards for SEO, established UX heuristics for Design, brand positioning frameworks for Brand, sector competitive analysis for Compete, and conversion-rate benchmarks for Growth.
