The Shift

The decade-old playbook has expired.

For a decade, the formula was simple: rank on page 1, generate clicks, work your funnel. That formula still holds for a shrinking slice of queries. For the rest, AI has already answered the question before your listing appears.

54% of UK adults now use AI tools — rising to 79% of 16–24-year-olds and 74% of 25–34-year-olds. 94% of B2B buyers use ChatGPT, Copilot, Perplexity, or Claude during the purchase journey. Your buyers are not opening five tabs to compare options. They are asking AI for the final answer.

UK AI Overviews launched on 15 August 2024. The UK has now been operating with AI-summarised search results for the better part of two years. The shift is not approaching. It is underway.

If your brand is not part of the AI answer, you don't exist in that buyer's world. Not because your product is inferior. Because your website is not structured to be read, understood, and cited by the systems making the recommendation.

This brief explains the mechanics — and gives you a framework to diagnose where your business stands today.

The AI Maturity Curve

Every business on the web falls into one of four categories.

The Agentic Web does not grade on a curve. Based on over a dozen structural and content citation-factor checks, every business currently sits at one of four rungs. The question is which one is yours.

The Mechanics

What moves you between rungs.

The Maturity Curve is not a spectrum of effort. Each transition between rungs requires a specific, identifiable change — and the changes are not evenly distributed in cost or complexity. Most businesses are stuck at Invisible or Emerging not because the fixes are expensive, but because nobody has told them which fix applies to them specifically.

Invisible → Emerging

Unlock the front door.

The most common cause of Invisible status is a robots.txt file that blocks AI crawlers — often set years ago during a GDPR or security hardening exercise and never revisited. GPTBot, ClaudeBot, and PerplexityBot must be explicitly permitted.

  • 01Audit robots.txt — permit GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, GoogleOther
  • 02Add one Organisation or LocalBusiness schema block
  • 03Ensure core facts (name, location, what you do, who you serve) exist as plain HTML — not inside JavaScript, images, or iframes

Typical effort: a single sprint. Zero budget required beyond developer time.

Emerging → Cited

Give AI something worth citing.

Emerging businesses are visible but unreliable. AI can find them but cannot accurately describe what they do or why they're different. The transition to Cited requires extractable, attributed, specific content — not more content, better-structured content.

  • 01FAQ schema on your most-queried pages — the highest-leverage single schema type for citation frequency
  • 02A named expert or founder bio with verifiable credentials, as plain text on the page
  • 03At least one attributed, specific statistic per key service page (the +41% citation uplift is real)
  • 04Pricing or service scope visible as HTML text — not hidden behind "contact us"

Typical effort: 2–4 weeks. Content-led, no significant rebuild required.

Cited → Recommended

Become the answer, not just a source.

Cited businesses appear when queried. Recommended businesses are selected — AI leads with their name, not alongside three competitors. The transition requires authority signals that extend beyond your own website.

  • 01H1s and opening paragraphs structured to answer likely AI queries directly — the first sentence is the citation
  • 02Active presence on the 3–5 platforms your sector's AI trust hierarchy uses as authority sources
  • 03Attributed client quotations on the page — AI treats named voice as a credibility multiplier (+28% citation frequency)
  • 04Consistent brand signals across every AI-indexed property — your own site, third-party profiles, trade press mentions

Typical effort: 60–90 day programme. The rung most businesses cannot reach without an audit first.

The problem is not which rung you're on. It's not knowing which specific fixes move you to the next one — for your site, your sector, your competitors.

The Evidence

You solved for User Experience.
Now you must solve for Agent Experience.

You have spent years making your website beautiful for humans. Human-beautiful websites are often invisible to AI. The mechanics of AI citation are documented — and they are not what most marketers expect.

AI crawlers operate on a token budget. If your site is bloated, renders late, or buries core facts inside JavaScript, the AI burns through its budget and leaves without understanding what you do. Your competitor — with a technically inferior product but a cleaner information architecture — gets cited instead.

A peer-reviewed study published at KDD 2024 (Aggarwal et al., Generative Engine Optimization) measured what actually increases a page's likelihood of being cited in a generative engine response. The findings are specific, replicable, and actionable.

FirstPageSage, 2025 · Perplexity ranking factors study

There is a further complexity most businesses have not yet confronted: different AI models cite different pages. A URL cited reliably in ChatGPT may be completely invisible in Perplexity, and vice versa. There is no single "AI visibility" — there is ChatGPT visibility, Perplexity visibility, Google AI Overviews visibility, and Gemini visibility, and they can diverge sharply by vertical. Monitoring requires model specificity, not a single score.

Two Problems Most Businesses Don't Know They Have

The bake-off is happening without you.

Beyond visibility, two specific failure modes are costing businesses enquiries and revenue — silently, before a buyer has ever visited their site.

The Vendor Bake-Off Problem
Enterprise buyers are asking AI to compare vendors directly — pricing, features, support. If your pricing is hidden behind "contact us," buried in a PDF, or locked inside an image, AI cannot include you in the comparison. You lose the bake-off silently, before the buyer has ever visited your site.
"Compare Vendor A and Vendor B on price, features, and customer support."
AI cannot include you in this answer if your data isn't cleanly extractable as HTML text. It will either hallucinate your features or skip you entirely — and recommend a competitor with a simpler information architecture.
The Citation Consensus Problem
AI does not only read your website. It reads everything the internet has already said about you — niche forums, sector publications, trade press, third-party review sites. For every vertical, there are specific platforms AI treats as authoritative sources.
For consumer queries: Reddit. For B2B services: specialist trade press and industry bodies. For hospitality: sector-specific review aggregators.
Being absent from the platforms your sector's AI trust hierarchy uses is as damaging as having a broken website. Most businesses have no idea which platforms matter for their specific vertical.
The Timing Question

The question is not whether this is happening. It is when the tipping point reaches your vertical.

AI platforms still drive a fraction of total internet traffic compared to Google. Google handles 14 billion searches a day; it is not going anywhere overnight. That is not the point.

The point is that AI Overviews are already eroding click-through rates on Google by 58% for the queries that trigger them. The businesses being cited in AI answers today are building a compounding advantage. Citation positions compound as models retrain and reinforce existing sources.

The businesses that are Recommended in AI results 18 months from now are the ones getting audited today. The question is not whether to act — it is whether you have the lead time to act before your vertical tips.
